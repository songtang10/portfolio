Executive Summary

For this project, our team worked with Daimler Truck North American and was tasked to design for Daimler’s Mission Control, a connected platform that will enable interaction with the self-driving truck. Several critical questions are being asked so that appropriate iterative transitions can be staged and deployed: will the functions of the dispatcher evolve and adapt to the new physical and cognitive challenges? What type of interactions and workflow exists as hardware and software advance? How does the current traditional dispatcher system transform and integrate with the future mission control system? And how do we leverage technology and human in tandem to achieve the desired results?

Background Research

Autonomous trucking involves many key stakeholders, and together they made up a complex system. As a team, to better understand the design problem space, we conducted background research and came up with a concept map for the current trucking system.

From our research, we identified a few major issue on the dispatcher’s side:

  • Chaotic and busy schedule when coordinating between different stakeholders
  • Need to deal with unexpected situations: scheduling, emergency, etc
  • Inefficient channel of communication, primarily phone calls
  • Lack of a central system to manage all the information

We then reimagined the future state of the autonomous trucking systems with a journey map. This further helped us to highlight pain points and design opportunities with autonomous trucking:

With Daimler’s Hub to Hub autonomous trucking system, the middle miles on high ways, where no drivers are present on the truck, is where we see the most need for Mission Control and remote dispatcher interaction. As a result, we formulated a How Might We Statment to help us develop a more concrete starting point for our design process:

How might we promptly communicate emergency situations on the highway using mission control?

Design Iteration

From this problem statement, we initially brainstormed for three possible directions:

For Dispatcher
Painpoints:
  • Handling lots of information simultaneously
  • Lack clear priority when dealing with emergency
  • Old-school communication channel
Mission Control's Role:

Track the Fleet in Real Time

Clear and efficient status update for all trucks

Automated Priority Assessment

Help dispatcher make better, more informed decision in providing help

Replay the Past

Verify truck status and confirm the shipment is safely completed.

For First Responders
Painpoints:
  • Hard for them to get familiar with a new truck in the first time
  • Figure out what’s wrong with the truck immediately
Mission Control's Role:

Inform & Give Access

Verify and inform the First Responders on site.

Provide Real-time Instruction

Allow first responders to interact with the vehicles in a visible and straightforward way.

Record & Collect Data

Record the process and relevant data and report back to the dispatchers.

For Other Drivers
Painpoints:
  • Lack of trust/awareness for autonomous trucks
  • High accidents rate between trucks and regular cars
Mission Control's Role:

Integrate with Existing Maps

Showcase the location of autonomous trucks on google maps.

Alert Other Drivers

When autonomous trucks encounter a breakdown, other drivers can see the warning on maps.

Asking for Help if Possible

Outsourcing to other drives on the road to assist with the emergency situation

After this initial round of brainstorming, we conducted more research and received feedback, which helped us to further narrow down to these first two levels of emergency handling: Incidence response and remote human interaction. And more specifically, we want to focus on the interaction between dispatchers and first responders.

Final Design

For this project, I was responsible for the final prototype design. Our final design-Elert, is an all-in-one platform that allows dispatchers to process unexpected emergencies in an efficient, effective, and elegant way. With data provided from truck sensors, Elert is able to provides truck status overviews, real-time emergency updates, and multi-channel communication between all involved stakeholders.

Detailed Features
1. All-in-one platform

Dispatchers will be able to view all the information at once. The summary statistics at table viewwill provide dispatchers with the truck status overview. By clicking on a specific entry, dispatchers can also see the detailed status of a specific truck, including load details and status updates.
Dispatchers can also switch to map view for a more location-centric status overview.
With such a platform, dispatchers can use the consolidated info to monitor their trucks in an efficient way.

2. Emergency Alert

Another important feature of the platform is the emergency alert notification. When an emergency is detected, the system will push notifications to the dispatcher and provide details such as location, truck status, and video footage. The push notification provides the dispatcher with priority so that they can deal with emergencies promptly and efficiently.
The recommended action provided by the system provides flexibility to the dispatchers as they can select and choose the action they want to take.

3. Seamless Verification Process

Other stakeholders such as technicians and police officers can interact with the interface effortlessly.
The technician will receive task notifications from their Elert app. When they arrive on-site, they can verify their identification by simply scanning the code. The process for the police is very similar: all they need to do is hold up their ID and look into the camera on the truck interface.
By providing a seamless verification process, Elert makes sure that the emergency situation can be dealt with smoothly.

2. Logges Timeline & Files

A highlight of Elert during the processing stage is its logged timeline and files. Whenever there is an update on any parties, the timeline will reflect the change. In this way, the dispatcher will be able to track the status and intervene if necessary. Files such as police reports and technician reports will also be available via the platform. This provides full transparency so that in case any legal issues come up later, the dispatcher can retrieve the logged information and provide evidence accordingly.
